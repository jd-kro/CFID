General Questions about CFID

Overview questions about CFID, who to contact, updates, etcc.

  • Question 1: What is CFID?

    Answer: The Comprehensive Freight Improvement Database (CFID) is a multifaceted database containing a rich array of attribute and spatial information about issues affecting goods movement in the Greater Tampa Bay Region. CFID is a web mapping application that was developed by the Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) to create and maintain a running spatial inventory of roadway and other transportation infrastructure issues and needs that impact regional goods movement and local accessibility to freight activity locations.

  • Question 2: Who operates and maintains the website?

    Answer: The Tampa Bay Goods Movement Team operates and maintains the website.

  • Question 3: Who is responsible for the maintenance an updates to CFID?

    Answer: The stakeholders including public transportation agencies, consultants, and freight and trucking firms.

  • Question 4: Who should I contact for additional information regarding CFID?

    Answer: Please contact us here: http://tampabayfreight.com/contact-us/

  • Question 5: Where does the data come from?

    Answer: The data in CFID originates from the provisional data phase provided by our stakeholders and experts who know and enter specific issues into CFID that are located on the freight network. The original CFID data originated from Freight Hotspots and by utilizing the District 7 (Citrus, Hernando, Pasco, Hillsborough, Pinellas counties) and District 1 (Manatee, Polk, Sarasota counties) Freight Corridor Reports, which analyzed state roadway conditions and issues. Consequently, items that were shown to be affecting and problematic for freight movement were standardized and entered into a database for further analysis and reporting.

  • Question 6: How are the projects prioritized?

    Answer: Prioritization is assessed by the relative importance of the issue. High Priority issues are on roadways that have known and obvious operational, capacity, maintenance, and/or safety issues. Prioritization is primarily based on relatively high level of truck traffic and congestion among other factors. Proximity, secondary roadways should be considered when assigning priority. Low Priority issues may be based on low truck volume roadways and proximity to freight activity centers and the operational conditions may not impede moderate to excessive amounts of freight movement.

  • Question 7: How is the Implementation Ease assigned to a project?

    Answer: Implementation Ease assigns the relative effort to implement the assessed improvement. Assigning the Implementation Ease requires reviewing the freight need and constraints. The greater the physical constraints the more difficult the Implementation Ease.

  • Question 8: How long does it usually take to implement an improvement?

    Answer: The time it takes to implement an improvement can vary greatly as many variables are considered. Some improvements can be resolved in a short amount of time, similar to Signal Timings or Landscaping/Sight Distance. Longer term or more constrained improvements can take much longer or possibly years to resolve through larger scale projects and assigned funding through work program.

Common Access Issues within CFID

  • Question 9: Do I need a username and password to access the site?

    Answer: No. The data is public, however, passwords are required to either contribute or edit the data. Contributors and editors have different roles. Contributors add provisional records, and comment on current records. The editors update the attribute information, provide data stewardship /progress, archive or remove completed records.

  • Question 10: Can the CFID data be shared with the public?

    Answer: Yes. CFID data is publically open and available. Please use the metadata when distrubuting the CFID data.

  • Question 11: What are the types of Users that use the system?

    Answer: Three types of user types are used in CFID. Public (read only), Contributor, and Editor.

  • Question 12: How do I get access as a contributor or editor to the system?

    Answer: Members of the GMAC will be regarded as primary contributors to CFID. You will be contacted by the Project Manager with username/password credentials.

  • Question 13: What are the differences between the privileges of a contributor and the editor profile?

    Answer: Contributor access is required in order to add a record. Once you log in to CFID, you will find the Add Record button in the lower left column. Click the button and a dialog window will open with all available selected value options.

  • Question 14: How can I get access to editing to the system?

    Answer: You will be contacted by the Project Manager with username/password credentials.

  • Question 15: I know of a project that should be included. How can I add this project?

    Answer: Contributor access is required in order to add a provisional record. Once you log in to CFID, you will find the Add Record button in the lower left column. Click the button and a dialog window will open with all available options. Many fields are required to add a provisional record, so please review inputs prior to submitting the record. Web Application Specific Tasks and Questions

Covers how to accomplish frequently performed tasks while using CFID.

  • Question 18: How do I query the data and how do the filters work?

    Answer: Expand and collapse filters by clicking on the filter heading. CFID utilizes an intuitive menu of expandable filters to enable users to quickly locate freight improvement issues by location, type, priority, ease of implementation, and a host of other variables. Multiple selections can be made within and across filters to retrieve CFID records matching any given set of criteria. Expanding the filter will display the categorical values associated with the heading, while collapsing a heading simplifies the display by hiding its underlying values. This enhances the overall legibility of the user interface and allows users to quickly locate the filter headings that most interest them. For any given heading, each value may be checked on or off to limit the results that will be returned by the filter tool to those that match the selected criteria. Multiple selections can be made under one or more headings. When selecting desired criteria, the grid view will dynamically update to display all matching records.

  • Question 19: How do I print a specific detailed record?

    Answer: Once a record has been identified, on the bottom panel there is a link named "More Info". Click on the link, which will open the details in a new browser tab, and is easily printable using keyboard "CTRL” + “P”

    Answer: The database is on a continuous update cycle and will reflect newly added records on a nightly basis. Once a new record has been added it will not show up until 12AM the next day.
